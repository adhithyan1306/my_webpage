⏳ The Grandfather Paradox

Changing the past erases the cause of the change itself.

If you go back in time and prevent your grandfather from meeting your grandmother, you are never born. But if you were never born, you could never have traveled back.

This creates a causal loop with no consistent outcome.

🔄 The Bootstrap Paradox

Information exists without ever being created.

A time traveler gives Shakespeare a copy of Hamlet. The play is copied, published, and centuries later bought again by the traveler.

Who wrote Hamlet originally? The information has no origin.

🚢 The Ship of Theseus

If everything changes, what stays the same?

Every plank of Theseus’s ship is replaced over time. Eventually, no original material remains.

🤥 The Pinocchio Paradox

A statement that cannot be true or false.

Pinocchio says: “My nose grows longer now.” If it’s true, it shouldn't grow. If it's false, it should grow, making it true.
