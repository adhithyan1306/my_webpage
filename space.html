The Great Unknown

Space is the ultimate frontier. It is vast, silent, and governed by laws that challenge our very understanding of existence. Here, time stretches, stars collapse, and light itself can be trapped forever.

Black Holes

Regions of spacetime where gravity is so strong that nothing—not even light—can escape. They are the ultimate "drain" of the universe.

Neutron Stars

The remnants of massive stars. They are so dense that a single teaspoon of neutron star material would weigh about a billion tons.

The Universal Limit: c

The speed of light (299,792,458 m/s) isn't just a speed; it's the speed of causality. As you approach it, time for you slows down relative to the rest of the universe (Time Dilation).

"If you travel at the speed of light, you could reach the moon in 1.3 seconds, but you'd need infinite energy to get there."

Dark Matter & Energy

Everything we see—planets, stars, galaxies—makes up only about 5% of the universe. The rest is Dark Matter and Dark Energy.

Supernovae

The violent death of a star. In its final moments, a supernova can outshine an entire galaxy and create the elements that make up our bodies.
